Featured Activities

Rome

Rome, Italy is the capital and most populous city in Italy, a historical and
cultural center renowned for its ancient ruins, Renaissance art, and iconic
landmarks like the Colosseum and Trevi Fountain. Situated on the Tiber River
and built upon seven hills, Rome is also home to Vatican City, an independent
country within its boundaries, and is known as the "Eternal City" for its lasting
legacy and enduring significance as a political, religious, and artistic hub.

Venice

Venice, Italy, is a capital city of the Veneto region in northeastern Italy,
famously built on a lagoon of 118 islands connected by canals and bridges,
where boats replace cars. Known for its rich history as a wealthy trading
empire and now a popular tourist destination

Colosseum

The Colosseum, an iconic elliptical amphitheater located in the center of Rome,
Italy, is the largest ancient amphitheater ever built, and the world's largest
standing one. Commissioned by Emperor Vespasian in 72 AD and completed
in 80 AD under Emperor Titus, it was known as the Flavian Amphitheatre
and could hold an estimated 50,000
to 80,000 spectators.
